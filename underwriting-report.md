## 6. REGULATORY & ZONING ANALYSIS

### CRITICAL: Rooming House License Required

Baltimore City requires a **rooming house license** for properties with 3+ unrelated tenants sharing common facilities. Without this license, operating a 4-room co-living unit violates city code.

| Requirement | Status | Cost/Action |
|-------------|--------|------------|
| Property Registration (DHCD) | Required within 10 days of title | $500 fine if late |
| Rental Dwelling License | Required -- $60/unit, 2-year term | Includes passing inspection |
| Rooming House License | **REQUIRED for 3+ unrelated tenants** | Confirm process with DHCD |
| Lead Paint Registration (MDE) | **MANDATORY (1923 build)** | $75 for 2-year period |
| Lead Paint Inspection | At every tenant turnover | $150-$300 per inspection |
| Zoning Approval | May need conditional use permit | Consult zoning attorney |

### Zoning

- Current designation: **R-7 or R-8 (Rowhouse Residential)**
- In R-7/R-8, conversion to multi-tenant requires **conditional use approval by City Council ordinance**
- 2 unrelated persons maximum without rooming house classification
- **Housing Options and Opportunities Act** (pending) would allow duplex/triplex/fourplex in R-7/R-8 if passed -- potential tailwind

### Lead Paint (Non-Negotiable)

Built in 1923 -- 100% subject to Maryland lead paint laws:
- Annual registration under owner-specific MDE Tracking Number
- Lead inspection at every tenant turnover (unless certified lead-free)
- All chipping/peeling paint must be corrected before certificate issued
- Budget: $2,000-$5,000 for lead-safe certification, $150-$300 per turnover inspection
